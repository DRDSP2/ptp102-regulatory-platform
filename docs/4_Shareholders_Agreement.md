## 1. DEFINITIONS

- **(a) “Company”** means Byrock Clinical Ltd and its subsidiaries or special-purpose vehicles related to the PTP-102 equine technology platform.
- **(b) “Shareholders”** means the persons/entities named in Schedule A.
- **(c) “Shares”** means the equity shares, preferred shares, or licence-interest units of the Company.
- **(d) “PTP-102 Technology”** means the equine clinical technology, including software, biomarkers (oltipraz, zofroxia, mogrosides, camel antibodies/PTP-100, cirsiliol, CB3700/CB3701, BioBarcode/WARF), regulatory dossiers, and associated IP.

---

## 2. SHAREHOLDING

- **2.1** The shareholding details are set out in **Schedule A**.
- **2.2** No shareholder can transfer shares except in accordance with this Agreement.

---

## 3. TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

- **3.1** No transfer of shares to any person without prior written consent of other shareholders.
- **3.2 Right of First Refusal (ROFR):** If a shareholder wishes to sell, the other shareholders have the right to purchase on the same terms.
- **3.3** Transfers require Board approval and may be subject to regulatory consent (EMA, FDA, veterinary device authorities).

---

## 4. BOARD OF DIRECTORS

- **4.1** The Board will consist of directors as agreed.
- **4.2** Matters requiring Board approval include:
  - (a) Issue of new shares or securities.
  - (b) Borrowing beyond **EUR/GBP/USD _________**.
  - (c) Sale of substantial assets related to PTP-102.
  - (d) Entry into material licence or collaboration agreements.
  - (e) Any other mutually agreed matter.

---

## 5. RESERVED MATTERS

Approval of not less than **_____%** of paid-up share capital required for:
- (a) Alteration of capital structure.
- (b) Change in line of business.
- (c) Merger, amalgamation, or sale of PTP-102 IP.

- (e) Any other matter as mutually agreed.

## 6. DIVIDEND
