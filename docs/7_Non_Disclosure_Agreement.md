## 1. PURPOSE

The Parties may disclose confidential information solely for evaluating a potential business relationship, regulatory collaboration, or technology licence agreement relating to the PTP-102 equine clinical platform.

---

## 2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

“Confidential Information” means any non-public information disclosed by either Party to the other, including:
- Regulatory submissions and correspondence (EMA, FDA, veterinary device authorities)
- Clinical trial data, biomarker results, and lameness-assessment algorithms
- Business plans, financial projections, and partnership terms
- Technical know-how, software code, and device specifications
- Marketing strategies and pricing models

Information marked as confidential or that should reasonably be understood to be confidential is covered.

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

The Receiving Party shall:
1. Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence.
2. Not disclose to any third party without prior written consent.
3. Use Confidential Information solely for the stated Purpose.
4. Restrict access to personnel with a legitimate need to know, bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations.

---

## 4. EXCLUSIONS

Information is **not** Confidential if it:
- Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party.
- Was lawfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure.
- Is rightfully received from a third party without confidentiality obligations.
- Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information.
