## 1. PURPOSE & SCOPE

### 1.1 Goal
Transition PTP-102 (methylated tirilazad / U-74389G) from preclinical / pilot-stage manufacturing through full CMC development, GMP production, and regulatory submission to achieve **FDA CVM Conditional NADA approval** by 3Q28, with aligned UAE early-access registration.

### 1.2 Scope Inclusions
- API sourcing strategy and vendor qualification
- Formulation lock (5 mg/mL sterile injectable, 0.2 μm filtration)
- Process development: route scouting, yield optimisation, impurity profiling
- Analytical method development, validation, and transfer
- Stability studies (accelerated + real-time)
- Specification establishment (release, shelf-life, in-process controls)
- GMP manufacturing of clinical batches (RXE and TAS)
- Regulatory CMC technical section writing (FDA, VICH, UAE MOCCAE)
- DECRS registration and maintenance for manufacturing / drug-product sites
- Toxicology support: genotoxic impurity assessment, residual solvents, elemental impurities
- Transfer of CMC documentation to license partner (if deal closes post-conditional approval)

### 1.3 Scope Exclusions
- Preclinical pharmacology / toxicology (already completed or subcontracted separately)
- Clinical study execution (ETCR site management, veterinary investigators)
- Commercial distribution logistics post-approval
- Patent prosecution (handled by Byrock IP counsel)
- Compounded product development (compounding-pharmacy pathway — explicitly excluded per regulatory advice)

## 5. STUDY DESIGN REFERENCE (LAM-00007 SILVER MOON PROTOCOL)

### 5.1 Real-World Safety Data
**Trial:** LAM-00007 (Byrock Veterinary Research | PTP-102 Laminitis Clinical Trial)  
**Subject:** Silver Moon — 10-year-old Thoroughbred mare, 535 kg  
**Laminitis Grade:** 3 (front right limb)  
**Protocol:** IV administration of PTP-102 at 1 mg/kg (535 mg per dose)  
**Dosing Schedule:** Every 8–24 hours; 7 total treatments over 72 hours  
**Investigators:** Dr. Jennifer Martinez, Dr. David Kim  
**Status:** Completed successfully; **no adverse reactions**  
**Outcomes by timepoint:**
- Hour 0: Initial administration
- Hour 8: No complications
- Hour 24: Reduction in pain response
- Hour 32: Improved mobility
- Hour 48: Continued positive response
- Hour 56: Excellent clinical response
- Hour 72: Treatment complete — favorable

**CMC Relevance:** The 1 mg/kg IV dosing regimen and 72-hour protocol window form the clinical baseline for GMP batch sizing and shelf-life justification.

### 5.2 Planned Formal Studies (HVC / ETCR)

| Study | Design | Animals | Groups | Dose Levels | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|--------|-------------|---------|

## 7. QUALITY & COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS (DETAILED)

### 7.1 FDA & VICH Standards
- **21 CFR Part 211** (CGMP for finished pharmaceuticals)
- **VICH GL1–GL60** (stability, validation, quality, safety)
- **ICH Q1A–Q2B1** (stability, validation)
- **ICH Q3D** (elemental impurities)
- **ICH M7** (genotoxic impurities)
- **FDA Guidance for Industry: CMC content for INDs and NDAs** (applied to CNADA structure)
- **FDA MUMS Guidance** (conditional approval provisions, fee waiver criteria)

### 7.2 Site Compliance
- All API and drug-product sites must maintain **active FDA DECRS registration** throughout development and post-approval
- **PIC/S** GMP certification acceptable for EU / UAE filings
- Annual GMP audit schedule (internal + byrock QA)
- CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action) system in place at manufacturing sites
- Temperature mapping and environmental monitoring for stability chambers (ICH Q1A)

### 7.3 Documentation Standards
- All development work documented in **GMP-compliant lab notebooks** (bound, paginated, dual signature)
- Batch manufacturing records (BMRs) reviewed by qualified person (QP) before release
- Audit trail requirements for electronic lab notebooks and chromatography data systems
- Change control: any formulation, method, or site change evaluated for regulatory impact before execution
